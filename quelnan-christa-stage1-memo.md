## Executive Summary

This memo proposes a comprehensive accounting ratio analysis of ExxonMobil Corporation (NYSE: XOM), one of the world's largest integrated oil and gas companies. The objective is to evaluate ExxonMobil's financial health across six ratio categories — Performance, Profitability, Efficiency, Leverage, Liquidity, and Du Pont — using data drawn from its most recent SEC EDGAR 10-K filing. Raw financial statements reveal what happened; ratio analysis reveals why and how well. This project will produce a working Excel model (Stage 2), a technical specification documenting model structure and methodology (Stage 3), and a final strategic memo with recommendations and a structured AI prompt (Stage 4). Findings will inform senior leadership's assessment of ExxonMobil's operational efficiency, capital structure, and competitive positioning within the energy sector.

## Company Background & Objectives

**Company:** ExxonMobil Corporation (NYSE: XOM)
**Industry:** Integrated Oil & Gas / Energy
**Description:** ExxonMobil is one of the world's largest publicly traded energy companies, engaged in the exploration, production, refining, and marketing of oil, natural gas, and petrochemical products across more than 60 countries.

The energy sector is capital-intensive, cyclically exposed to commodity price swings, and increasingly scrutinized for balance sheet resilience. A single fiscal year's income statement or cash flow statement tells only part of the story. Ratio analysis allows us to contextualize ExxonMobil's performance relative to its own historical trends and industry benchmarks — revealing whether the company is generating adequate returns on its assets, managing debt responsibly, and maintaining the liquidity needed to weather commodity downturns.

This analysis will directly inform internal discussions around capital allocation, risk exposure, and operational efficiency — questions that are central to any CFO's mandate.

## Approach

Financial data will be sourced from ExxonMobil's most recent Annual Report on Form 10-K, filed with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission via SEC EDGAR (https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar). Supplemental data may be cross-referenced against Yahoo Finance and ExxonMobil's Investor Relations page (https://investor.exxonmobil.com) for consistency checks.

The analysis will be organized across six ratio categories:

1. **Performance Ratios** – Measure how effectively the company generates revenue and earnings relative to key benchmarks such as assets and equity.
2. **Profitability Ratios** – Assess margins at the gross, operating, and net income levels to evaluate cost management and pricing power.
3. **Efficiency Ratios** – Evaluate how productively ExxonMobil deploys its assets and manages working capital, including asset turnover and inventory cycles.
4. **Leverage Ratios** – Examine the company's debt load, interest coverage, and capital structure to gauge financial risk and long-term solvency.
5. **Liquidity Ratios** – Determine whether ExxonMobil holds sufficient short-term assets to meet its near-term obligations without stress.
6. **Du Pont Analysis** – Decompose Return on Equity (ROE) into its constituent drivers — net margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage — to pinpoint the root causes of performance changes over time.

Results will be computed in a structured Excel model using direct inputs from ExxonMobil's consolidated financial statements, with clearly labeled formulas and source references.

## Limitations & Next Steps

**Known Constraints:**
- Financial data is drawn from audited annual filings and reflects historical performance; ratios do not predict future results.
- Industry benchmarks used for comparison are approximations, as peer-group composition varies by source.
- ExxonMobil's 2024 acquisition of Pioneer Natural Resources significantly altered the company's balance sheet, which may limit direct year-over-year comparability on certain metrics.
